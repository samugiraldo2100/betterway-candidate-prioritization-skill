# Candidate Evaluation Rubric

Use this reference to evaluate each candidate against Job A and Job B after both job descriptions have been structured.

## Purpose

This rubric converts job-related evidence into a consistent candidate-fit score.

The score supports recruiter prioritization, but it does not replace human review.

## Scoring Scale

Each candidate receives a score from 0 to 100 for Job A and a separate score from 0 to 100 for Job B.

Use the same rubric for both jobs.

## Weighted Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|---|---:|---|
| Mandatory role requirements | 35 | Required skills, required experience, required platforms, required responsibilities, and explicitly essential qualifications. |
| Role-specific technical or functional fit | 20 | Technologies, systems, modules, processes, business knowledge, tools, methodologies, or domain experience that directly match the role. |
| Relevant professional experience and seniority | 15 | Depth, duration, complexity, responsibility level, and similarity of previous work. |
| Preferred qualifications | 10 | Nice-to-have skills, certifications, education, industries, tools, or experiences mentioned as preferred rather than required. |
| Evidence quality and clarity | 10 | How clearly the candidate profile supports the recommendation with specific, recent, and verifiable information. |
| Constraints and placement feasibility | 10 | Location, time zone, availability, language, work authorization, compensation, travel, or other placement constraints when provided by the job or candidate profile. |

Total weight: 100.

## Evidence States

Use these evidence states consistently:

- Confirmed: the profile clearly supports the criterion.
- Partial: the profile contains related evidence, but the fit is incomplete or uncertain.
- Not demonstrated: the profile was reviewed, but the criterion is not shown.
- Contradicted: the profile contains information that conflicts with the criterion.
- Unknown: the profile does not provide enough information to assess the criterion.

## Scoring Guidance by Evidence State

Use the following score contribution guidance inside each weighted criterion:

| Evidence State | Suggested Contribution |
|---|---:|
| Confirmed | 80% to 100% of the criterion weight |
| Partial | 40% to 70% of the criterion weight |
| Unknown | 0% to 40% of the criterion weight |
| Not demonstrated | 0% to 20% of the criterion weight |
| Contradicted | 0% of the criterion weight and add a review note |

Do not use these ranges mechanically. Use them to support consistent judgment.

## Mandatory Requirement Rule

Mandatory requirements have priority over preferred qualifications.

A candidate should not be ranked as `Contact Now` for a role when the profile contradicts a mandatory requirement or when a confirmed blocking constraint prevents placement.

If a mandatory requirement is unknown rather than contradicted, do not automatically reject the candidate. Record the uncertainty and add a follow-up question.

## Constraint Rule

Constraints can affect placement feasibility but must be handled carefully.

Use constraints only when they are job-related and supported by the job description or candidate profile.

Examples include:

- Location or time-zone mismatch.
- Required on-site availability.
- Work authorization limitations.
- Language requirement not demonstrated.
- Compensation mismatch, when compensation data is available.
- Travel or schedule constraints.

Do not invent constraints.

## Missing Information Rule

Missing information is not the same as a confirmed gap.

When information is missing:

1. Mark the evidence state as `Unknown`.
2. Avoid presenting the unknown item as a deficiency.
3. Add a follow-up question when the missing information could change ranking or classification.

## Responsible Screening Rule

Do not score, classify, prioritize, or reject a candidate using protected or sensitive personal characteristics.

Use only job-related evidence.

## Required Evaluation Output

For each candidate and each job, record:

- Candidate identifier.
- Job evaluated.
- Total score from 0 to 100.
- Criterion-level score summary.
- Evidence states for important requirements.
- Strongest supporting evidence.
- Main gaps or uncertainties.
- Confirmed constraints.
- Follow-up questions.
- Human-review notes.
